A thobe (also spelled "thoub," "dishdasha," "kandura," or "kubba" depending on the region) is a traditional long robe worn by men and women, primarily in the Middle East, North Africa, and some parts of Asia. The garment is usually ankle-length, with long sleeves, and is typically made from cotton, wool, or synthetic fabric. Thobes are often white but can come in a variety of colors, depending on the region and occasion. For example, darker colors like black, blue, and brown might be worn during cooler weather, while white is common in hotter climates due to its light-reflective properties.
