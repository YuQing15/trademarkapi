The mark consists of the stylised wording “TAKE TEN PERCENT” presented in bold, three‑dimensional lettering. The word “TAKE” appears at the top in white block letters with a blue outline. Beneath it, the word “TEN” appears in orange and yellow gradient block letters, accompanied to the right by a stylised “10%” in matching colours with a downward red arrow behind it. The word “PERCENT” appears below in white block letters with a blue outline. Below the wording are four stylised icons arranged horizontally: (1) a blue alarm clock above the word “TIME”; (2) a red‑and‑white calendar page displaying “10 DAYS”; (3) an orange‑and‑white calendar page displaying “10 MONTHS”; and (4) a globe wrapped with a golden banner displaying “10 YEARS”. The background is royal blue with radial rays and a semi‑transparent clock face. The mark is used in connection with trauma‑informed programmes, educational services, wellness workshops, social‑impact initiatives, printed and digital materials, and community‑based empowerment services.

Take Ten Percent has three words that are common in other trademarks but not used like mine. There is already Take Ten or Ten Percent but not as Take Ten Percent (TTP). No claim is made to the exclusive right to use the words “TEN,” “PERCENT,” “TIME,” “DAYS,” “MONTHS,” “YEARS,” or the numerical expressions “10,” “10%,” “DAYS,” “MONTHS,” “YEARS,” or generic depictions of clocks, calendars, arrows, or globes apart from the mark as shown. Protection is claimed for the unique stylisation, arrangement, colour scheme, and overall commercial impression of the mark.
